The Disappearing Art Forms of Rwanda

Vanishing Traditions

Traditional Rwandan art forms like Imigongo paintings and Intore dance are disappearing rapidly, especially in rural districts (Huye, Musanze, and Nyagatare) due to modernization, lack of documentation, and youth disengagement.

Key Challenges

  • Oral transmission of knowledge with elder practitioners passing away
  • Urban migration and globalization accelerating the decline
  • Lost potential for cultural tourism ($620M industry in 2023)

Our Hybrid Preservation Model

Digital-Physical Hub

We combine an online archive of endangered art with mobile workshops co-created with local artisans to preserve Rwandan cultural heritage while creating economic opportunities.

Reflections & Future Vision

Challenges & Lessons

Intergenerational Knowledge Transfer

We learned that documenting oral traditions requires deep community trust and innovative recording methods.

Technology Adoption

Rural youth showed great enthusiasm for digital tools when connected to cultural preservation.

Sustainable Models

Combining preservation with income generation proved essential for long-term engagement.
